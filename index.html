LRC and MEHE Collaborate to Strengthen Education Sector Preparedness

  • The Lebanese Red Cross (LRC) and the Ministry of Education and Higher Education (MEHE) have joined forces to bolster the education sector's preparedness and response capabilities, particularly in anticipation of potential conflict escalation. This collaborative effort encompasses various initiatives, including training programs, shelter assessments, and a comprehensive evacuation plan.

Support Resources and Ongoing Collaboration:

  • LRC-MEHE Partnership: The ongoing partnership between LRC and MEHE extends beyond emergency preparedness. It aims to strengthen school disaster management mechanisms by focusing on infrastructure, coordination, and governance.
  • Vulnerability and Capacity Assessment: Identifying potential disaster risks faced by schools.
  • Essential Preparedness Training: Equipping school personnel with vital skills like firefighting, first aid, and rescue techniques.
  • Evacuation Planning: Developing and practicing efficient evacuation procedures.
  • Contingency Planning: Formulating comprehensive plans to address various emergency scenarios.
  • Curriculum Integration: Advocating for the integration of DRR concepts and curriculum within the education system.

MEHE Hotline:

The existing MEHE hotline (01/77200) remains operational, offering health-related support to schools in Lebanon. LRC DRR unit are continuously Operating the call center during hours between 8 AM to 6 PM, but extend to 24/7 during critical situations to provide uninterrupted assistance.

Collaborative Response Plan:

  • LRC-Led Training: The LRC-DRR unit will continue to lead training initiatives for MEHE personnel, focusing on:
    • Armed Conflict Scenarios: Equipping staff with knowledge and skills to navigate conflict situations effectively.
    • Evacuation Plans: Developing and practicing evacuation procedures to ensure the safety of students and staff during emergencies.
    • Red Zone Awareness: Highlighting specific safety measures for schools located in high-risk areas (red zones).
  • School Asset Inventory: An inventory will be conducted to identify available resources within schools located in designated red zones. This data will be shared with relevant units to facilitate resource allocation in times of crisis.
  • Data Management: Data and information pertaining to schools will be scanned, archived, and duplicated to ensure accessibility in emergency situations.
  • Academic Continuity Solutions: The plan proposes two potential solutions for ensuring academic continuity during emergencies:
    • Online Learning: Implementing online learning platforms to minimize disruption to education.
    • School Enrollment Coordination: Coordinating with educational districts to facilitate student enrollment in the nearest available schools if displacement necessitates temporary relocation.

Enhancing Preparedness Through Training:

  • Supervisor Training: LRC-DRR spearheaded training sessions for MEHE health and environment supervisors in public schools. These sessions covered crucial topics like armed conflict awareness, evacuation planning, and drills, empowering them to lead preparedness efforts within their schools.
  • Online Training: LRC expanded its reach by conducting online training modules for educational institutions, ensuring a wider pool of prepared personnel.

MEHE Inventory and LRC Safety Assessments:

  • MEHE Inventory: The Ministry provided a list of available schools that could potentially be used as collective shelters during large-scale displacement events.
  • LRC Safety Assessments: In close coordination with the DRM unit, LRC DDR Unit's technical team conducted thorough safety assessments on these identified school buildings to ensure their suitability for sheltering displaced populations.

Evacuation Planning Guide for Educational Institutions:

Building on the collaborative efforts, the LRC developed a comprehensive document outlining the evacuation process for educational institutions. Supporting the Relevant Ministries "MoPH"

Minister of Public Health's Tour and Key Decisions

The Minister of Public Health, Dr. Firas Abiad, completed his southern tour on November 7, 2023, where he inspected hospitals in Tyre, displaced persons shelters, and the Disaster Management Unit of the Union of Tyre municipalities, and assessed the readiness to counter any emergency. Furthermore, he closely reviewed the details of displaced populations, their locations, and the aid extended to them. During the visit, the minister made significant decisions, such as activating the emergency services of Qana Hospital to cater to the needs of the displaced, ensuring a resolution within 48 hours for facilitating childbirth for pregnant women, and activating the hotline 1787 to allow displaced persons to request medications promptly.

The LRC in close coordination with UNICEF and MoPH has initiated and completed the children routine immunization campaign through Mobile Medical Units- MMUs in targeted areas in Tyre with high number of displaced persons.

On November 9, the Disaster and Crisis Management Committee met in the North Governorate. The latest developments related to preparedness for current situations in Lebanon were addressed. Several key points were discussed during the meeting, including Dr. Suboon's presentation of the Ministry of Public Health's plan on the importance of preparing for any emergencies or developments in light of the current situation, as well as an assessment of hospitals and their categorization (red, yellow, and green). Representatives of the Lebanese Red Cross suggested suitable centers as shelters for displaced individuals in the region. The meeting concluded with the agreement to hold subsequent smaller meetings, including one with local associations in Tripoli, one for coordinating field operations, and one with district governors to coordinate crisis cells and the governorate's emergency operations room. Additionally, action points were outlined, such as sending a list of Civil Defense needs to the UNDP for comprehensive research and ensuring effective provision, as well as Crisis and Disaster Management Room coordinating with hospitals to gather necessary information for preparedness.

Supporting the Relevant Ministries "MOIM"

Visit and Discussions

On November 2, 2023, a visit was conducted by the Ministry of Interior and Municipalities, accompanied by the Disaster Risk Reduction (DRR) unit and the Disaster Risk Management Unit (DRMU) at the Grand Serail. This visit included the South and Nabatieh Governorates, concluding it with a tour at the Union of Tyre Municipalities Emergency Operation Rooms.

The primary focus of the discussions was the need to centralize coordination and collaborative efforts through the emergency operation rooms in the governorates, working closely with the Ministry of Interior and Municipalities and the DRMU. Emphasis was placed on the importance of the electronic platform designed for automating data collection and presenting it through dashboards and relevant tools. Furthermore, the crucially and effective role of the Lebanese Red Cross, especially in their capacity for developing surveys, managing data, and utilizing dashboards, was highlighted. It was also stressed that organizing and coordinating all NGOs through the administrative structure of the emergency operating rooms is essential to avoid duplication and ensure clear roles and services for each NGO. This approach aims to better address people's needs and enhance preparedness and response efforts.

The visit to Nabatieh and the South Governorate showcased the support and ongoing preparedness and response work carried out by the DRR team of the Lebanese Red Cross, with detailed explanations and demonstrations of the dashboards and tools in use. The Ministry gained valuable insights into the ongoing efforts in both governorates, including a visit to one of the designated shelters in Tyre, where members of the Union of Tyre Municipalities were also present.

The LRC is putting effort into centralizing coordination and collaborative efforts through the emergency operation rooms in the governorates, working closely with the Ministry of Interior and Municipalities and the DRMU. Daily reports on internally displaced persons are being provided to the minister.

Capacity Building for Governorates

Training Initiatives

The DRR trainers, in close collaboration with the DRM Unit at the Presidency of the Council of Ministers and the governors, conducted a training session on the "proper activation of the emergency operation room" for members of the DRM committee in the governorates. This training was part of preparedness activities to ensure an efficient and timely response in case of an emergency. The DRM committee includes representatives from various ministries, the Army, Civil Defense, General Security, media agencies, and other relevant stakeholders.

A training session on "National Disaster Management Plan" took place in the Southern Governorate - Saida. The training aimed to enhance preparedness and response in emergency situations and improve the capabilities of relevant teams.
